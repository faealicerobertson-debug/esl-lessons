Retail Investors: From Meme to Mainstream

Guided reading and speaking lesson based on: “How retail investors went from meme to mainstream”

Morning Brew · Finance / Markets · Reading + Speaking Practice

1. Before Reading

Goal: First, understand the article. Then, use the article to speak about investing, risk, young investors, and Chile.

You do not need to understand every word. Focus on the main ideas.

Warm-Up

  • Do you invest money?
  • Do young people in Chile talk about investing?
  • What is safer: saving money or investing money?
  • What is riskier: stocks, crypto, or private companies?

2. Vocabulary

Match the word with the meaning.

Word Meaning
retail investor a normal person who invests their own money
checking account a normal bank account for everyday money
stock a small piece of ownership in a company
outperform to do better than something else
invest to put money somewhere to make more money
go public to begin selling shares on the stock market
risk the possibility of losing money
private company a company that is not on the public stock market

Spanish Help

  • stock = acción
  • share = acción / participación
  • risk = riesgo
  • investment = inversión
  • profit = ganancia
  • loss = pérdida
  • private company = empresa privada
  • go public = salir a bolsa

3. Article Walk-Through: Paragraph 1

“The class of investors that chose a dog wearing a tie as its avatar during the original GameStop days now looks and acts more like your parents.”

What does this mean?

The writer is talking about retail investors.

In the past:

  • many retail investors were young
  • many used memes
  • many used social media
  • people did not always take them seriously

Today:

  • they are acting more seriously
  • they are investing more strategically
  • people are paying more attention to them

Say it in your own words

Use this sentence:

“In the past, retail investors were seen as ________, but today they seem more ________.”

4. Article Walk-Through: Paragraph 2

Retail investors beat the S&P 500 last year.

  • The retail investor stock basket gained 30.5%.
  • The S&P 500 gained 16.4%.

Simple Explanation

The article says that individual investors actually made more money than the market average.

This challenges the idea that retail investors are “dumb money.”

Say it in your own words

“According to the article, retail investors performed better than ________. This is interesting because ________.”

5. Article Walk-Through: Paragraph 3

Many young adults are moving money from checking accounts into investment accounts.

In 2015:

  • only 6% of 25-year-olds did this.

In 2024:

  • more than 33% did this.

What is the trend?

More young people are investing instead of only keeping money in the bank.

Possible reasons:

  • social media
  • easy investing apps
  • inflation
  • interest in financial independence
  • fear of missing opportunities

Say it in your own words

“More young people are investing because ________. In Chile, I think this trend is ________.”

6. Article Walk-Through: Paragraph 4

Popular stocks include:

  • Nvidia
  • Amazon
  • Reddit
  • AppLovin
  • Palantir
  • Tesla

The article also mentions SpaceX.

SpaceX is still private, but it may become public soon.

Simple Explanation

Retail investors often like famous technology companies. These companies feel exciting because they are connected to AI, space, social media, or innovation.

Say it in your own words

“Retail investors are interested in tech companies like ________ because ________.”

7. Article Walk-Through: Paragraph 5

Some investors already invest in SpaceX through SPVs.

SPV = Special Purpose Vehicle

An SPV allows groups of people to invest in private companies.

Simple Explanation

Normally, you cannot easily buy SpaceX stock because it is not public.

An SPV is one way investors try to get access.

Spanish Help

SPV = vehículo de propósito especial

It is a structure that allows investors to put money into a specific investment.

Say it in your own words

“An SPV can help investors access ________, but it can also be risky because ________.”

8. Article Walk-Through: Paragraph 6

The article says SPVs can be:

  • complicated
  • difficult to understand
  • risky
  • opaque

Sometimes investors do not know exactly where their money is going.

Simple Explanation

The article warns that some investments may look exciting, but they can be hard to understand. If an investor does not understand the structure, the investor may not understand the real risk.

Say it in your own words

“The main problem with SPVs is ________. In finance, transparency is important because ________.”

9. Article Walk-Through: Final Idea

Warren Buffett says that buying or selling one-day options is not investing.

What is the warning?

The article ends with a warning: some retail investors may be investing seriously, but others may be acting more like gamblers.

Say it in your own words

“The difference between investing and gambling is ________.”

10. Speaking Questions

Answer these questions in complete sentences. Try to speak for 30–60 seconds per question.

A. Understanding the article

  1. What is the main idea of the article?
  2. How have retail investors changed?
  3. Why does the author mention GameStop?
  4. Why is the performance of retail investors surprising?

B. Your reaction

  1. What surprised you most?
  2. Do you think retail investors are becoming smarter?
  3. Do you agree that retail investors are now more respectable?
  4. What is missing from the article?

C. Chile and personal experience

  1. Is investing popular among young Chileans?
  2. Do people in Chile trust the stock market?
  3. What kinds of investments are common in Chile?
  4. How is Chile similar to or different from the United States?

D. Finance professional opinion

  1. What mistakes do retail investors often make?
  2. What is more dangerous: not knowing enough, or being too confident?
  3. Should risky investments be more regulated?
  4. Where is the line between investing and gambling?

Speak for 2 minutes.

Try to include:

  • one summary of the article

  • one risk
  • one personal opinion
  • one piece of advice for young investors
